# A Semantically Consistent Dataset for Data-Efficient Query-Based Universal Sound Separation

arXiv entry：[https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22599](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22599)

PDF：`files/2601.22599v1.pdf`

## Abstract

Query-based universal sound separation is fundamental to intelligent auditory systems, aiming to isolate specific sources from mixtures. The paper identifies a data bottleneck in in-the-wild datasets: weak labels and severe event co-occurrence can lead models to learn spurious correlations between background noise and target categories instead of robust acoustic features.

The paper proposes a pipeline that mines high-purity single-event segments from in-the-wild datasets and synthesizes mixtures via a semantically consistent protocol. It constructs Hive, described in the arXiv abstract as a high-quality synthetic dataset comprising 2.4k hours of raw audio. The abstract reports competitive separation accuracy and perceptual quality compared with a much larger SAM-Audio training set, plus zero-shot generalization on out-of-distribution benchmarks.
